Insomnia

Insomnia is a sleep disorder characterized by difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, or waking up too early and not being able to fall back asleep. It can be caused by stress, anxiety, depression, or certain medications.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty falling asleep
  • Waking up frequently during the night
  • Feeling tired and not refreshed after sleep
  • Difficulty concentrating during the day

Prevention

  • Establish a regular sleep schedule
  • Create a relaxing bedtime routine
  • Avoid caffeine and electronics before bedtime
  • Manage stress and anxiety
⚠ Recommended to consult with a doctor for further check-ups

Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. It can be of two types: obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) and central sleep apnea (CSA). OSA occurs when the throat muscles relax, while CSA happens when the brain fails to send proper signals to the muscles that control breathing.

Symptoms

  • Loud snoring
  • Episodes of breathing cessation during sleep
  • Gasping for air during sleep
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness

Prevention

  • Lose weight if overweight
  • Avoid alcohol, sedatives, and sleeping pills
  • Use a CPAP machine (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) to keep airways open during sleep
  • Sleep on your side instead of your back
⚠ Recommended to consult with a doctor for further check-ups
